UC Berkeley 2024-2025

Open CAL
(Computed Axial Lithography)
A research project

The goal of this project:
To bring CAL technology to the maker community.

Computed Axial Lithography or CAL is a new type of additive manufacturing that is layerless. The CAL technology has been maturing in research labs for half a decade, and it is finally at the point for widespread adoption. CAL has been shown to print parts over 100x faster than conventional 3D printers, and because all parts are fabricated at once, layering drawbacks such as lower strength and poor surface finish are eliminated. CAL also enables new manufacturing techniques such as overprinting, where parts can be fabricated over existing structures, and it works with a wide range of new materials with great physical and mechanical properties. Best of all, every aspect of the technology will be open source, allowing for crowdsourcing of innovation, while enabling the users to fully understand their printers.

UC Berkeley 2024

Walking Robot
Using One Motor

The goal of this project:
To achieve a walking robot that can walk forward or backward using only one motor.

The robot employs a crank-slider mechanism to induce an elliptical trajectory for its legs, enabling smooth and energy-efficient locomotion. Synchronization of all four legs is achieved through a worm gear-driven axle system, minimizing mechanical complexity. The robot prototype, weighing 403g, features modular 3D-printed components for iterative testing and future adaptability.

UC Berkeley 2024

Vibration Dampening Pen
To regain legible handwriting.

The goal of this project:
To create a pen that can resist a shaky hand while writing.
This is achieved by using gyroscopic forces to create a dampening effect.

This pen uses a metal ring embedded into a plastic disk to create gyroscopic motion. It spins by using a brushless motor and its accompanying electronics which are stored in the pen. When a person's hand shakes, it applies a moment onto the pen which is then counteracted by the gyroscopic force allowing for dampening of the tremor.

UC Riverside 2024

Wave Energy Generator

The goal of this project:
To harness energy from ocean waves with a near-shore buoy.

By utilizing the oceans waves we are able to collect clean renewable energy. This is achieved by using a buoy that moves up and down along a stationary rack which, by turning a DC motor with a pinion attached to the shaft, generates energy from the waves which can be used back on land.
